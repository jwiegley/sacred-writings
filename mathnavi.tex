\section*{Translator's Note}

This translation of Bahá'u'lláh's Mathnavi employs archaic reverential English in the tradition established by Shoghi Effendi, Guardian of the Bahá'í Faith. The use of ``Thou,'' ``Thee,'' and ``Thy'' with capitalization when addressing the Divine reflects the elevated register of the Persian original and maintains consistency with authorized translations of Bahá'í sacred texts.

\vspace{1em}

The 318 couplets (bayts) presented here constitute a complete mystical poem revealed by Bahá'u'lláh, the Prophet-Founder of the Bahá'í Faith. Following the classical Persian masnavi form, the work explores themes of divine love, spiritual transformation, and mystical union.

\vspace{1em}

For glossary definitions of Persian and Arabic terms, please consult the accompanying \textit{Mathnavi Glossary}.
